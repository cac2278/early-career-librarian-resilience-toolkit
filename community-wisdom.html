Community Wisdom Archive

Wisdom From Librarians Who Have Been There

This archive is being built one reflection at a time.

It will eventually contain short reflections from librarians, archivists, MLIS students, library staff, and information professionals who have navigated rejection, burnout, uncertainty, isolation, and professional change.

Community Wisdom Coming Soon

The goal of this archive is simple:

To preserve the kinds of things people often learn the hard way and make them available to the next person who needs them.

Many of the experiences discussed throughout this toolkit can feel isolating, even though they are widely shared.

This archive will collect small pieces of wisdom from people who have lived through those experiences and want to help others feel less alone.

Help the Next Librarian Feel Less Alone

We are currently accepting submissions for the archive.

You do not need to write a story.

One honest sentence is enough.

Examples:

Confidence came back much later than competence.

Rejection hurt less when I stopped treating it as evidence.

Burnout was not proof that I cared. It was proof that I was exhausted.

All submissions will be reviewed before appearing on the site.
