Why We Created This Community

One of the goals of this toolkit is not only to provide information, but to reduce the isolation that often accompanies early-career librarianship.

Many of the experiences discussed throughout the toolkit are easier to navigate when they can be discussed with people who understand them.

Professional uncertainty, rejection, burnout, imposter feelings, workplace disappointment, and loss of confidence are often treated as private struggles.

They do not have to remain private.

We hope this community becomes a place where people can find conversation, support, and perspective during difficult periods of professional life.

Help the Next Librarian Feel Less Alone

We are building a Community Wisdom Archive: a collection of short reflections from librarians, archivists, MLIS students, and information professionals who have lived through difficult professional experiences.

Many people arrive at this toolkit feeling isolated in experiences that are actually widely shared.

Your reflection may help someone understand that they are not the only person who has struggled with rejection, burnout, uncertainty, loss of confidence, or professional disappointment.

You do not need to write a story.

One honest sentence is enough.

All submissions will be reviewed before appearing on the site and may be edited lightly for clarity, formatting, length, or anonymity.
