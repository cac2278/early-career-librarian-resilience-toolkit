I Feel Disconnected from Librarianship

A short page for moments when the values that brought you here feel distant or difficult to access.

A Note From the Toolkit Creators

We know how frightening it can feel when librarianship once felt meaningful and now feels far away.

You may still believe in access, education, preservation, public service, intellectual freedom, or community care. But the profession itself may feel exhausting, alienating, or emotionally unreachable.

That disconnection can feel like failure.

It may also be grief.

Before You Decide What This Means

Burnout and precarity can make meaning temporarily inaccessible. Work that once felt purposeful can begin feeling distant when it becomes tied to rejection, instability, overwork, or institutional disappointment.

Alienation does not always mean your values have disappeared. Sometimes it means the conditions around the work have made those values harder to feel.

The question may not be whether you still care. It may be what kind of conditions would allow care to become sustainable again.
