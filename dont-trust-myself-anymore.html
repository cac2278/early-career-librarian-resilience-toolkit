I Don't Trust Myself Anymore

A short page for moments when institutional outcomes have started to feel more believable than your own lived experience.

A Note From the Toolkit Creators

We know how destabilizing it can feel when rejection or instability starts making you doubt your own judgment.

You may look at your accomplishments and feel detached from them. You may wonder whether mentors were just being kind, whether you misread your abilities, or whether everyone else can see something you cannot.

That kind of self-doubt can become exhausting.

It does not mean you have become unreliable. It may mean you have spent too long being evaluated under unstable conditions.

Before You Decide What This Means

Chronic evaluation and uncertainty can erode self-trust. Over time, external outcomes can begin feeling more authoritative than internal evidence.

A rejection may feel more real than years of demonstrated competence. A hiring committee may feel more believable than your own record of work, care, and growth.

Self-trust often returns gradually. It usually begins by gathering evidence again, not by forcing confidence before you feel it.
