I Feel Emotionally Isolated at Work

A short page for moments when the workplace continues as usual while your internal world feels anything but usual.

A Note From the Toolkit Creators

We know how painful it can be when professional disappointment becomes relational disappointment too.

Sometimes the outcome hurts. Sometimes what hurts more is watching people avoid the subject, perform normalcy, or fail to acknowledge that something painful happened.

That kind of silence can make you question not only the workplace, but the relationships you thought you had inside it.

Wanting acknowledgment is not unreasonable.

Before You Decide What This Means

Workplaces often reward emotional containment. People may avoid difficult moments not because your pain is invisible, but because they do not know how to meet it.

That does not make avoidance harmless. It can still be lonely, destabilizing, and disappointing.

This experience may be telling you something important about where you feel safe, where you feel watched, and which relationships can hold more than professional politeness.
