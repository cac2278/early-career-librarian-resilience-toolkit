I'm Exhausted But Can't Stop Working

A short page for moments when rest feels necessary but also strangely unsafe.

A Note From the Toolkit Creators

We know what it feels like to be exhausted and still feel unable to stop.

Under precarity, rest can start to feel dangerous. It can feel like falling behind, losing legitimacy, disappointing people, or confirming the fear that you were not capable enough in the first place.

So you keep going, even when your body and mind have been asking for relief for a long time.

That is not sustainable. It is also not a personal defect.

Before You Decide What This Means

Burnout often develops when people remain in prolonged states of demand, uncertainty, emotional labor, and self-monitoring.

In vocational fields, exhaustion is often moralized. People are taught to treat overextension as commitment and boundaries as selfishness.

But exhaustion is not proof of dedication. It is information.

Your body may be telling the truth before your professional identity is ready to hear it.
