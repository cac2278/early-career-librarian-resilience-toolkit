I Got Rejected

A short page for moments when non-selection feels less like an outcome and more like a verdict.

A Note From the Toolkit Creators

We know that rejection can hurt far beyond the email, phone call, or search outcome itself.

Sometimes the hardest part is not only losing the opportunity. It is losing the version of the future that had started forming around it.

It can feel especially painful when you cared deeply, prepared carefully, and allowed yourself to hope.

That hope was not embarrassing. It was human.

Before You Decide What This Means

Rejection often expands under precarity. It can start as “I was not selected” and quickly become “there is no place for me.”

That expansion is emotionally understandable, especially when work, income, belonging, and identity are all entangled.

But hiring outcomes are shaped by many things you cannot see: budgets, timing, institutional politics, internal needs, and committee dynamics.

Rejection can reveal a real loss. It cannot reveal your whole future.
