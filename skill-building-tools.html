Skill-Building Tools

Practical tools for translating experience, building language, and moving with more clarity

These tools are designed for people navigating early-career librarianship, career transitions, job searching, rejection, uncertainty, and professional identity work.

They are meant to help you name what you already know, recognize transferable skills, and build usable language for next steps.

Skill-building does not always mean starting from zero.

Many people entering librarianship already bring experience in teaching, customer service, research, organizing information, outreach, technology, supervision, writing, care work, project coordination, or community support.

The challenge is often learning how to translate that experience into the language of library work without minimizing where you came from.

Use These Tools If...

You are trying to describe your experience, prepare for interviews, revise a resume, process a difficult professional moment, or understand how your previous work connects to librarianship.
